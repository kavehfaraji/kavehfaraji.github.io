    PAPERS:

    Kaveh Faraji Googerdchi, Shahrokh Asadi, Seyed Mohammad Bagher Jafari
    Status: In press (Submitted: December 2023, Accepted: May 2024, Published: June 2024)
    Target Journal: PLOS ONE (eISSN: 1932-6203)

    Customer churn modeling in telecommunication using a novel multi-objective evolutionary clustering-based ensemble learning;

    One of the primary challenges’ organizations face is customer churn. Accurate customer churn prediction can protect organizations from high costs and lead to their growth. One of the most widely used methods for predicting churn is ensemble learning models. Diversity and prediction performance are two essential principles for constructing ensemble classifiers. Therefore, developing accurate ensemble learning models consisting of diverse base classifiers is a considerable challenge in this area. For churn prediction in this study, two models of multi-objective evolutionary ensemble learning based on clustering (MOEEC)s; are proposed, which are include a novel diversity measure. Also, to overcome the data imbalance problem, another objective function is presented in the second model to evaluate ensemble classifiers performance. The proposed models in this paper are evaluated with a dataset collected from a mobile operator database. The results indicate that the proposed models are superior to previous models based on four performance metrics: Accuracy, AUC, F-score, and G-means.

    Journal PDF

    Hossein Azimi, Kaveh Faraji Googerdchi,
    Status: SSRN working paper (Posted: 23 April 2022)
    Target Journal: SSRN Electronic Journal

    Investigating the Effective Factors on Mobile Bank Acceptance According to the Unified Theory of Acceptance and Use of Technology (Utaut)

    Mobile Bank is one of the service platforms that banks want to fully implement, but its realization largely depends on customer motivation and acceptance. The purpose of this study is to investigate the factors affecting behavioral intention and mobile bank acceptance. This research is applied in terms of purpose and descriptive survey in terms of data collection method. The statistical population was the customers of Qazvin Province Bank. The data collection tool was a questionnaire that was distributed among 387 customers. Cronbach's alpha reliability coefficient was confirmed with 0.956 and convergent validity with AVE more than 0.4. Hypothesis testing and model fit were performed by structural equation modeling and pls method. According to the findings, the effect of behavioral intention and facilitation conditions on mobile bank acceptance was confirmed. The effect of pleasure motivation, social influence, trust, performance expectations and satisfaction on behavioral intention was also confirmed.
